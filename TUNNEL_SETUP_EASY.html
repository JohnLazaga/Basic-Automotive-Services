Open your shop app from anywhere

A very simple guide to making a "tunnel" so you can open a branch's app from home or your phone — not just at the shop. Follow it slowly.

What is this? (in kid-simple words)

Right now your shop app only works at the shop, on the shop Wi-Fi. A tunnel is like a safe, secret straw that reaches from the internet to the mini-PC at your shop. Once the straw is set up, you can open the app from your house, or your phone on mobile data, from anywhere in the world.

We use a free tool called Cloudflare to make the straw.

Before you start: the mini-PC must already be set up and running the app (from the "Easy Branch Setup" guide). Do everything below on the mini-PC.

There are two ways. Start with the Easy Way.

Easy Way — a temporary link (safe, 5 minutes)

This makes a link you can use today to check the app from your phone. It changes nothing on your website and can't break anything. The link is temporary — great for trying it out.

1 Open the black typing window

On the mini-PC, press the Windows key, type cmd, and press Enter. A black window opens (the "Command Prompt").

2 Install the tunnel tool (once)

Type this and press Enter. Wait for it to finish (a minute or two):

Type this, then Enterwinget install --id Cloudflare.cloudflared
If it says it's already installed, that's fine — carry on.

3 Turn on the tunnel

Type this and press Enter:

Type this, then Entercloudflared tunnel --url http://localhost:8790
What you should see: after a few seconds, a web link appears that looks like
https://some-random-words.trycloudflare.com

4 Use your link

  • Open that https://…trycloudflare.com link on your phone (turn Wi-Fi off to prove it works on mobile data!). You'll see the shop's sign-in page.
  • Keep the black window open — the link only works while it's open.
Remember: this link is temporary. It changes every time you run Step 3, and stops when you close the window or restart the mini-PC. Perfect for testing. For a permanent link that never changes, do the Permanent Way below.

Permanent Way — a proper link like pilot.basicautomotiveservices.com

Please read this first. This way changes settings for your real website (basicautomotiveservices.com). If a setting is done wrong, your main website can stop working for a while. It is not hard, but go slowly — and it's best to do it with someone helping, or ask us to hop on a quick call and do it together. There is no rush; the Easy Way above works in the meantime.

Here is what happens, in plain words (the exact commands are in the technical guide, TUNNEL_SETUP.md):

1 Make a free Cloudflare account

Go to cloudflare.com and sign up (it's free). Then click "Add a site" and type basicautomotiveservices.com.

2 Keep your old settings

Cloudflare will show a list of settings it found for your website. Do not delete any of them. These keep your current website alive. (Leave them marked "DNS only" — a grey cloud icon.)

This is the part where mistakes cause problems. If unsure, take a photo of the list and send it to us before changing anything.

3 Point your website name to Cloudflare

Cloudflare gives you two "nameserver" addresses. You type these into the company where you bought your website name (your domain seller). Save. Then wait — Cloudflare will say "Active" when it's ready (can take from a few minutes to a few hours).

4 Connect the mini-PC to Cloudflare

Back on the mini-PC's black window, you sign the tunnel tool in to your Cloudflare account, create a tunnel named after the branch, and point the address pilot.basicautomotiveservices.com at it. (Four short commands — see TUNNEL_SETUP.md, or do it with us.)

5 Make it always-on

One last command tells the tunnel to run by itself, even after restarts. Now the branch is reachable at https://pilot.basicautomotiveservices.com from anywhere.

Now that it's on the internet — stay safe

  • Change the admin password (never leave it as admin).
  • Give staff strong passwords.
  • Optional extra lock: Cloudflare has a free feature called Access that asks for an email code before anyone even sees the sign-in page. Ask us to switch it on if you'd like.
